• Japanese Language

    I self-taught myself Japanese to fluency. I started learning Japanese in 2007. I'd been interested in Japan for a long before that but in 2007 I set my new year's resolution to study the language. I challenged myself first by studying the basics online and then getting grammar books from Japan (in Japanese), which increased my understanding of the language structure and vocabulary. I later started playing video games in Japanese and watching tv shows with subtitles in Japanese. I consider myself to be fluent and even when I don't know a word I can express myself using other words. I love 漢字!.

  • JLPT N2

    I earned JLPT N2 back in 2011. JLPT stands for Japanese Language Proficiency Test and N2 is the second most difficult(N1 being the highest level). I plan on sitting N1 one day just to test myself. I consider this my personal achievement as I never had a teacher to teach me this.

  • Scholarship

    In 2011 I went to Japan for 2 weeks on a scholarship for outstanding students of Japanese. I was the representative of Poland and met people from other 63 countries who also, just as me, were studying Japanese. We travelled around Japan learning the language, customs and history. I made loads of friends and I still keep in touch with them.

  • Participated in Karate tournaments

    Although I never really made it too far I consider taking part in various karate tournaments an achievement. Just preparing for those is challenging enough. I once got a 3rd place in a national 'Challenger' tournament taking place in my hometown - Opole. I also tried internationally twice - but with no results.

  • PCs

    I consider being able to work with computers to be my personal achievement as I have no difficulty understanding them and feel comfortable with them. I spend most of my days in front of PCs and being able to earn money by doing so is filling me with joy.
